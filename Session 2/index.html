About Dolphins

Oceanic dolphins (Dolphinidae) are a widely distributed family of dolphins that live in the sea. Close to forty extant species are recognised.

They include several big species whose common names contain "whale" rather than "dolphin", such as the Globicephalinae (round-headed whales including the orca and pilot whale).

Features

Dolphins have smooth, rubbery skin and are usually colored in some mixture of black, white, and gray. They have two flippers, or fins, on their sides, as well as a triangular fin on the back. Like other whales, they have an insulating layer of blubber (fat) beneath the skin.
